Carmel Mountain Ranch sits in a sweet spot between the I-15 corridor and the rolling hills to the east, making it one of San Diego's most desirable family neighborhoods. Whether you are near the Carmel Mountain Ranch Recreation Center or closer to the shopping at Carmel Mountain Plaza, fast service is always close.

Most CMR homes were built in the late 1980s and 1990s during the community's initial development. These well-built homes feature quality construction, but after three decades, HVAC systems and built-in appliances begin to show their age. We frequently repair and replace central air conditioning units, furnaces, and water heaters in CMR homes. The two-story floor plans common throughout the neighborhood can create uneven heating and cooling between floors -- a problem our technicians are skilled at diagnosing and resolving.

The Carmel Mountain area's inland position means it experiences wider temperature ranges than the coast. Summer afternoons regularly hit the mid-90s, while winter nights can dip into the low 40s. That range puts real demand on your heating and cooling equipment. Homeowners who walk the trails around Stonebridge Parkway or the kids' sports fields at the rec center know how quickly conditions change -- and they count on working AC when they get home.
