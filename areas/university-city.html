HVAC & Appliance Repair in University City

Fast, affordable service for the busy community surrounding UCSD and Westfield UTC

University City is one of San Diego's most densely populated and diverse neighborhoods, home to UC San Diego students, young professionals working in the biotech corridor along Towne Centre Drive, and established families in the single-family homes south of Governor Drive. A-Tech Repair serves all of University City, from the high-rise condominiums near Costa Verde and Westfield UTC to the townhome communities along Genesee Avenue and the established residential streets off Regents Road. Whatever your living situation, we have the experience to get your HVAC or appliances back in working order quickly.

The housing mix in University City creates a wide variety of service needs. Apartment and condo residents near the UTC mall area often deal with wall-mounted AC units, compact stackable washers, and shared HVAC systems that require a technician who understands multi-unit building configurations. Meanwhile, homeowners in the neighborhoods east of Genesee Avenue typically have traditional central air systems and full-size appliance suites. A-Tech technicians are comfortable working in both settings and carry parts for the brands and system types most common throughout University City.

University City sits in a transitional climate zone between the coast and the inland valleys. Summer temperatures here average 5 to 10 degrees warmer than La Jolla, which is just a few miles west, but stay cooler than neighborhoods like Mira Mesa or Scripps Ranch to the east. This means your air conditioning works moderately hard from June through October, and your heating system gets regular use during the cooler months when the marine layer pulls back and nighttime temperatures dip into the 40s. Keeping both systems properly maintained avoids the emergency calls that come when a neglected unit fails on the first hot day of the year.

If you rent near campus or own a condo in the Costa Verde area, we also handle the smaller-scale repairs that keep your compact appliances and through-wall units running right.
